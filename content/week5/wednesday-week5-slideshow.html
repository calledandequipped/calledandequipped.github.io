Finding Mentors and Coaches

"Walk with the wise and become wise" - Proverbs 13:20

Types of Mentors You Need:

  • Spiritual Mentor: Guides your faith journey
  • Industry Expert: Has succeeded in your field
  • Life Coach: Helps with personal development
  • Peer Mentor: Slightly ahead on similar path
  • Reverse Mentor: Younger person with fresh perspective

Qualities of Great Mentors

Choose your mentors wisely

Fruit in their life Alignment with your values Willing to be honest Available and accessible Celebrates your wins Challenges your growth Models what they teach Invests without agenda

Remember: Mentors are guides, not gods

Creating Accountability Structures

Weekly Check-ins

Regular meetings with accountability partner
Share wins, struggles, and next steps

Goal Partners

Someone pursuing similar objectives
Mutual encouragement and progress tracking

Prayer Partners

Committed to praying for your purpose
Spiritual warfare support

Mastermind Groups

Small group of purpose-driven peers
Collective wisdom and resources

The Importance of Spiritual Covering

"Obey your leaders and submit to them" - Hebrews 13:17

What spiritual covering provides:

  • Protection through prayer and wisdom
  • Correction when you're off course
  • Confirmation of God's direction
  • Connection to greater resources
  • Credibility through endorsement

Find a church/leader who champions your purpose

Identify Your Purpose Dream Team

Map out these key relationships:

  1. Mentor: Who's where you want to be?
  2. Coach: Who can train specific skills?
  3. Accountability Partner: Who will keep you on track?
  4. Prayer Warrior: Who will intercede for you?
  5. Encourager: Who believes in your dream?
  6. Truth-Teller: Who will be honest with you?
  7. Connector: Who can open doors?

This Week's Action Steps

Building Your Support System:

1. List 10 potential mentors in different areas

2. Research their work and values alignment

3. Craft personalized outreach messages

4. Reach out to at least 3 this week

5. Join one community aligned with your purpose

6. Schedule coffee with a potential accountability partner

Remember: Be specific about what you're asking for!
