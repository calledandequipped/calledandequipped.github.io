God Is Still Speaking!

Our God is not silent

  • He desires to communicate with His children
  • He speaks in multiple ways, not just one
  • Learning to hear requires practice and patience
  • Every believer can hear God's voice

Today: Discover the many ways God speaks to guide you into purpose

1. Through His Word

"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness"

- 2 Timothy 3:16-17

  • The Bible is God's primary voice
  • It never contradicts His character
  • Specific verses can leap off the page
  • Themes emerge through consistent reading
  • Foundation for testing all other words

2. Through the Holy Spirit

"When he, the Spirit of truth, comes, he will guide you into all the truth."

- John 16:13

The Spirit speaks through:

  • Inner promptings and impressions
  • Dreams and visions
  • Words of knowledge or wisdom
  • Deep inner peace or unrest
  • Illumination of Scripture

Biblical Examples

God spoke uniquely to each person

  • Moses: Burning bush and face-to-face
  • Samuel: Audible voice in the night
  • Elijah: Still small voice after the storm
  • Joseph: Dreams and interpretations
  • Paul: Visions and revelations
  • Peter: Trance and vision on rooftop

God meets us where we are!

Practice: Lectio Divina

Ancient practice of sacred reading

1. Lectio (Read)

Read slowly, savoring each word

2. Meditatio (Meditate)

Reflect on what stands out

3. Oratio (Pray)

Respond to God about what you've heard

4. Contemplatio (Contemplate)

Rest in God's presence

Try this with Psalm 139 this week!

Your "God Speaks" Journal

This Week's Assignment:

  1. Start a dedicated journal for hearing God
  2. Each day, record:
    • Scripture that spoke to you
    • Inner promptings or impressions
    • Circumstances that seem significant
    • Words from others that resonate
  3. Look for patterns and confirmation
  4. Test everything against Scripture
