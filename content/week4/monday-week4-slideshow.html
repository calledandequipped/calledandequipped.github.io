Fear: The Purpose Killer

Fear is the enemy of destiny

  • Fear keeps more people from their purpose than any other factor
  • It disguises itself as wisdom, caution, or being "realistic"
  • Fear creates paralysis when God calls for action
  • Every hero of faith had to conquer fear

Today: Learn to recognize, confront, and overcome the fears blocking your purpose

Identifying Fear-Based Decisions

Signs you're operating in fear:

  • Avoiding opportunities that stretch you
  • Making excuses about timing or readiness
  • Focusing on what could go wrong
  • Comparing yourself to others constantly
  • Waiting for "perfect" conditions
  • Staying in your comfort zone

"Fear says 'What if I fail?' Faith says 'What if I fly?'"

The Israelites at the Edge of Promise

Numbers 13-14: When fear cost a generation their destiny

Lessons from their failure:

  • 10 spies saw giants; 2 saw God's promise
  • Fear is contagious and spreads quickly
  • Fear causes us to forget God's past faithfulness
  • Fear leads to rebellion and wandering
  • What we focus on grows larger

Which report are you believing about your purpose?

Common Fears That Block Purpose

Fear of Failure

"What if I try and fall short?"
"What if I'm not good enough?"
"What if I disappoint God/others?"

Fear of Success

"What if I can't handle it?"
"What if people expect too much?"
"What if I lose myself?"

Fear of Rejection

"What if people don't accept me?"
"What if I'm criticized?"
"What if I lose relationships?"

Fear of the Unknown

"What if I make the wrong choice?"
"What if God stops providing?"
"What if I miss His will?"

Faith versus Presumption

Walking in wisdom while conquering fear

TRUE FAITH

  • Based on God's Word
  • Confirmed by peace
  • Submitted to timing
  • Open to counsel
  • Produces fruit

PRESUMPTION

  • Based on emotion
  • Driven by anxiety
  • Impatient with process
  • Resists accountability
  • Leads to confusion

Faith takes risks; presumption is reckless

Deliverance from Fear

It's time to break fear's hold on your destiny

Steps to Freedom:

  1. Identify: Name your specific fears honestly
  2. Confess: Bring them into the light (1 John 1:9)
  3. Renounce: Break agreement with fear
  4. Replace: Fill your mind with truth
  5. Act: Take one faith step today

"For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind." - 2 Timothy 1:7
